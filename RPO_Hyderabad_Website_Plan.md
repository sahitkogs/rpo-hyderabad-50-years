# RPO Hyderabad — 50 Years of Service
## Website & Telugu FAQ Video Project Plan

**Prepared for:** Jonnalagadda Snehaja, IFS — Regional Passport Officer, Hyderabad
**Project Goal:** Build a commemorative website and Telugu FAQ video library to mark 50 years of the Regional Passport Office, Hyderabad (1976–2026), and to make passport services more accessible to citizens.
**Document Version:** 1.0 — Initial plan for prototype handoff to Claude Code / Claude Design.

---

## 1. Project Overview

### 1.1 Why This Project
The Regional Passport Office, Hyderabad was established in 1976 after bifurcation from the Chennai Passport Office. In 2026, it completes 50 years of public service. This is a natural moment to:
- Celebrate the office's history and achievements.
- Showcase the citizen-centric reforms of recent years.
- Reduce friction for Telugu-speaking applicants through native-language video FAQs.
- Strengthen public trust through transparency and outreach.

### 1.2 Deliverables
1. **Commemorative Website** — A public-facing single-page (or small multi-page) site celebrating 50 years.
2. **Telugu FAQ Video Library** — Short-form videos (60–120 seconds each) addressing the most common citizen queries.
3. **Content Hub** — Easy-to-navigate sections covering history, services, achievements, and grievance redressal.

### 1.3 Success Criteria
- Citizens can find answers to top 10 passport questions within 30 seconds of landing on the site.
- Videos are accessible in Telugu, with English subtitles for wider reach.
- The site reflects the dignity and professionalism of a Government of India institution while being warm and approachable.
- Mobile-first: most users will access via phone.

## 3. Key Achievements & Initiatives (2024–2025)

These are the headline accomplishments to feature prominently. **All are sourced from credible news outlets — see Section 11 for full citations.**

### 3.1 National Recognition
- **June 2025:** RPO Hyderabad won the national award for *"Innovative Steps and Citizen-Centric Initiatives Undertaken during 2024–25"* at the Regional Passport Officers' Conference in New Delhi.
- Award presented by **Pabitra Margherita, Minister of State for External Affairs**, on the 13th Passport Seva Diwas (24 June 2025).
- Telangana Police separately felicitated for excellence in passport verification (received by Battula Shivadhar Reddy, IPS, DG Intelligence).

### 3.2 Citizen-Centric Reforms
- **Thursday Walk-in Grievance Redressal:** Every Thursday, 9:30 AM–12:30 PM, citizens can walk in without appointment. Over 30,000 people served in person in a single year.
- **Online Inquiry Appointments:** 250 slots released every Monday, Tuesday, and Friday.
- **Personal Email Review:** RPO personally reviews ~100 emails received daily.
- **Email resolution:** 10,000+ complaints resolved via email.

### 3.3 Operational Efficiency
- **Appointment wait time reduced from 22 days (2023) to 1–10 days for regular applicants.**
- **Tatkal appointments:** 1–5 working days.
- General passports delivered in 5–7 working days (excluding police verification).
- Tatkal passports issued within 1–3 working days.
- Daily processing: ~4,200–4,500 applications across the Telangana network.
- **9.02 lakh applications resolved in 2023** (combined passport + Police Clearance Certificate).
- 6.5 lakh passports issued in 2022; 3.5 lakh by mid-June 2023.
- Speed Post delivery: 3,000 passports dispatched daily, 24-hour delivery.

### 3.4 Technology & Modernization (Passport Seva 2.0)
- **e-Chip Passports launched March 2025** — embedded chip for enhanced security and faster immigration.
- **Online portal upgraded to Version 2** — easier application flow, integrated chatbots, clear document checklists.
- Mobile passport services — special van deployed for outreach and accessibility.

### 3.5 Civic & Cultural Initiatives
- **Commemorative Passport Envelope** released for 75 years of the Indian Constitution.
- **Year-long Art and Calligraphy Exhibition** on the Constitution of India, inaugurated by the Governor of Telangana.
- **Swachhata (Cleanliness) Campaign** that revitalised office premises.
- **Cyber awareness campaigns** for citizens.
- **Medical / Health camps** for staff and visitors.
- **Environmental drives** including rainwater harvesting at the office.
- **Outreach drives** for special groups (senior citizens, persons with disabilities).
- **Special Hajj counters** during Hajj season.
- **Emergency passport issuance** within hours for medical/death-abroad cases with genuine proof.

### 4.3 Design Direction
- **Tone:** Dignified, official, but warm — not bureaucratic.
- **Colour palette:** Indian tricolour as accent (saffron, white, green) with deep navy or maroon as primary. Avoid pure red/orange overload.
- **Typography:** Clean serif for headings (e.g., Playfair Display, Lora) paired with humanist sans-serif for body (Inter, Source Sans). Telugu script support: use Noto Sans Telugu or Mandali.
- **Imagery:** Office photos, staff at work, citizen interactions (with consent), historical photographs if available.
- **Iconography:** Simple line icons — no clip-art.
- **Government emblems:** MEA logo, Ashoka emblem must be used per government brand guidelines.

## 5. Telugu FAQ Video Plan

### 5.1 Format
- **Length:** 60–120 seconds each
- **Style:** Talking head (an officer or volunteer) + on-screen text/graphics
- **Language:** Telugu primary; English subtitles burned-in or as caption track
- **Hosting:** YouTube (embed on website); also share via @rpohyderabad social channels

### 5.2 Top 12 FAQ Topics (Phase 1)
1. **How do I apply for a new passport?** — Step-by-step on passportindia.gov.in.
2. **What documents do I need?** — Address proof, date-of-birth proof, photographs.
3. **What is Tatkal and when should I use it?** — Eligibility, fees, timelines.
4. **How long does a passport take?** — Realistic timelines, including police verification.
5. **How do I track my passport application?** — File number, online tracking, SMS alerts.
6. **What is the appointment process?** — Booking, rescheduling, walk-in vs online.
7. **What is Thursday Walk-in?** — Who qualifies, what to bring, what to expect.
8. **Renewal vs Re-issue — what's the difference?**
9. **Lost passport — what now?** — Police FIR, application steps.
10. **Minor's passport — what's special?** — Parents' documents, consent rules.
11. **Police verification — what happens?** — Pre/post verification, what police check.
12. **e-Passport — what changed?** — How the new chip works, what citizens should know.

### 5.3 Production Considerations
- A simple smartphone setup with a clip-on mic and good lighting can produce quality videos. No big budget required.
- Use a consistent intro/outro animation (5 seconds) with RPO Hyderabad branding.
- Pin a "playlist" on the YouTube channel.
- Future Phase 2: Hindi versions, Urdu versions for inclusivity.

## 6. Technical Build Plan

### 6.1 Recommended Stack
**For prototype (fastest path):**
- **Static site:** HTML + Tailwind CSS + a sprinkle of vanilla JavaScript
- **Hosting:** GitHub Pages, Netlify, or Cloudflare Pages (all free for this scale)
- **Domain:** Subdomain under an existing MEA/passport domain if approved, otherwise something like `rpohyderabad50.in` (≈₹500–₹1,500/year)
- **Videos:** YouTube embed (no hosting cost)
- **Fonts:** Google Fonts (free)

**For a more mature build (if scope grows):**
- **Framework:** Next.js or Astro (better SEO, easy CMS integration later)
- **CMS (optional):** Sanity, Strapi, or even Markdown files in the repo
- **Analytics:** Google Analytics or Plausible

### 6.2 Why This Stack
- Zero/low recurring cost.
- Fast to build (1–2 weeks for prototype).
- Easy to hand off to a future maintainer.
- Government-friendly: simple, auditable code.

### 6.3 Approvals & Compliance Checklist (Critical)
Before going live publicly with MEA branding:

- [ ] Accessibility audit (WCAG 2.1 AA — important for a government-facing site)
- [ ] Hosting on a `.gov.in` or `.nic.in` subdomain if officially adopted

## 8. Risks & Considerations

- **Branding rights:** Use of Government of India / MEA / Ashoka emblem requires permission. Until granted, use neutral commemorative branding.
- **Scope creep:** Resist the urge to make this a full service portal — the official application site (passportindia.gov.in) already exists.
- **Maintenance:** Decide who owns the site after launch — RPO IT staff? A volunteer? Plan for at least quarterly updates.
- **Privacy:** Do NOT collect any personal data (names, application numbers, etc.) on this site. Direct all such queries to the official portal or grievance channels.
- **Political neutrality:** Keep tone non-political. Focus on institutional service, not individuals as political figures.
- **Telugu accuracy:** Have native Telugu speakers review all video content and translations before publishing.

## 10. Hand-off Notes for Claude Code / Claude Design

If passing this plan to **Claude Code**:
- Build target: a single-page responsive website using HTML + Tailwind CSS + minimal JS.
- Mobile-first layout. Use CSS variables for the colour palette so it can be tuned.
- Use semantic HTML (`<header>`, `<section>`, `<nav>`, `<footer>`) — this is a government-adjacent site and accessibility matters.
- Section IDs should match the anchor nav (`#history`, `#achievements`, `#videos`, `#contact`).
- Placeholder all images with descriptive alt text and a `/assets/placeholder.jpg` until real assets arrive.
- Keep the file structure minimal: `index.html`, `styles.css` (or inline Tailwind), `script.js`, `/assets/`.

If passing this plan to **Claude Design**:
- Design tone: dignified, citizen-friendly, not corporate, not gaudy.
- Reference benchmarks: India.gov.in (for restraint), Singapore Government Design System (for clarity), MyGov India (for Indian context).
- Explore both a "modern editorial" direction and a "civic festive" direction; let the RPO team pick.
- Provide both desktop and mobile mockups; mobile is primary.
- Telugu typography must be tested at body and headline sizes — many Latin-first designs break with Telugu.
