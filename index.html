Our Achievements

Numbers that tell the story.

A snapshot of the citizen-impact metrics that define RPO Hyderabad's recent years.

  • 9.02 lakh Applications resolved in 2023 (passport + PCC)
  • 22 → 1–10 days Appointment wait time reduced dramatically since 2023
  • 30,000+ Citizens served via Thursday walk-in grievance redressal in a single year
  • e-Passport Chip-embedded passports launched in March 2025
  • National Award For "Innovative Steps and Citizen-Centric Initiatives" at Passport Seva Diwas, June 2025
  • Year-long Art & Calligraphy Exhibition on the Constitution of India

Innovations & Reforms · 2024–2025

Citizen-first changes, made everyday.

  • Thursday Walk-in Grievance Redressal

    Every Thursday, 9:30 AM – 12:30 PM, citizens can walk in without an appointment to have their grievance heard. Tens of thousands have been served.

  • e-Chip Passports & Passport Seva 2.0

    Embedded chip passports for stronger security and faster immigration; the online portal upgraded with clearer flows and integrated chatbots.

  • Mobile Passport Van

    A dedicated van takes passport services on the road for outreach drives, special groups, and accessibility-focused camps.

  • Cyber Awareness Campaigns

    Regular drives to help citizens spot scams, protect their documents, and use the official portal safely.

  • Swachhata & Rainwater Harvesting

    A revitalised office premises and on-site rainwater harvesting. Small habits, sustained over time.

  • Constitution @75 Envelope

    A commemorative passport envelope released to mark 75 years of the Constitution of India.

  • Art & Calligraphy Exhibition

    A year-long exhibition on the Constitution, inaugurated by the Governor of Telangana.

  • Personal Email Review

    The RPO personally reviews ~100 emails received daily; over 10,000 complaints resolved through email.

From the Regional Passport Officer

A note to the citizens we serve.

Hyderabad, 2026

For five decades, this office has had one job: to help the people of our region cross borders for work, family, study, and faith, with dignity, speed, and trust. The 50-year milestone is a moment to thank the citizens who have placed that trust in us, and the staff, past and present, who have honoured it.

This commemorative site is a small way to share what we have built together, and a promise that we will keep listening, keep improving, and keep showing up.

Jonnalagadda Snehaja, IFS Regional Passport Officer, Hyderabad
